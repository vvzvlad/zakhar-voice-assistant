# Shifting the Recall ↔ Music-False-Accept Pareto Frontier for a Tiny «Захар» Wake-Word Model

## TL;DR
- **The single biggest lever you have not fully pulled is negative-data scale and composition, not loss functions or architecture.** The reference open-source models reach their low false-accept rates because they are trained on tens of thousands of hours of negatives that include large amounts of music — openWakeWord's bundled models use ~31,000 hours (per its docs: ~10,000 h noise/music/speech from ACAV100M, ~10,000 h Common Voice 11, ~10,000 h podcasts from Podcastindex, ~1,000 h music from the Free Music Archive); microWakeWord's official models are "benchmarked so that on the DipCo set, they have at most 0.16 false accepts per hour and have less than 0.1 false accepts per hour on the PicoVoice benchmark." Your pre-generated `kahrendt/microwakeword` features (FMA-medium music + FSD50K + WHAM + CHiME6/DiPCo + LibriSpeech/VOiCES) are good but you should add far more *continuous music* hours (MUSAN music, MTG-Jamendo, FMA-large) sampled as `_ambient`/`split` clips, and mine hard music false-triggers. This shifts the whole frontier outward rather than trading one axis for the other.
- **For a fixed short 2-syllable word, the frontier is real but movable**: add a VAD pre-gate (already supported in the ESPHome component) plus a light two-stage "successive refinement" (speech?→keyword-like?→keyword) which the literature shows cuts false alarms by up to 7–8× on 13K–2.4M-param models without retraining the phrase, and tune the streaming post-processor (sliding_window_size, probability_cutoff, refractory) specifically against a continuous-music test set.
- **Confusable hard-negatives + more positive variety (real recordings ≫ more TTS voices) are the two highest-ROI data moves**; weight confusables at high penalty but moderate sampling so recall is not killed (community evidence: sampling 8.0 / penalty 5.0 works; pushing negative_class_weight too high, e.g. [60,75], measurably drops both recall and FAPH).

## Key Findings

**1. How the official low-FAPH models actually did it (data scale, not tricks).** The pre-generated negative feature sets on Hugging Face (`kahrendt/microwakeword`) are composed as: `no_speech_background` = FMA-medium (music) + FSD50K + WHAM; `dinner_party_background` = CHiME6 (train) + DiPCo (test); `speech_background` = LibriSpeech train-other + VOiCES. The comparable openWakeWord project documents that its bundled models "were all trained with ~30,000 hours of negative data representing speech, noise, and music," with per-model docs giving the exact ~31,000-hour composition: ~10,000 h of noise/music/speech from ACAV100M, ~10,000 h from Common Voice 11, ~10,000 h of podcasts from Podcastindex, and ~1,000 h of music from the Free Music Archive. microWakeWord's official models hit "at most 0.16 false accepts per hour" on DiPCo and "less than 0.1 false accepts per hour on the PicoVoice benchmark" (OHF-Voice/micro-wake-word Releases). The HF dataset card does **not** publish exact hour counts per source. The takeaway: your music-FA problem is overwhelmingly a *coverage* problem — your model has seen far less music diversity than the reference models.

**2. The frontier is fundamentally tied to phrase length/phonetic distinctiveness.** Multiple industry sources converge: SoundHound recommends 4–5 syllables ("the longer it is, the easier it is to differentiate… the fewer false acceptances"); Picovoice and others recommend 2–4 syllables with distinctive phonemes; a Google patent (US9275637, "Wake word evaluation") formalizes a "false alarm score metric" measuring the phonetic distance of a candidate's phone sequence to phone sequences in a large speech corpus — and notes adding syllables increases distinctiveness. «Захар» (Z-A-KH-A-R, 2 syllables, ~5 phonemes) sits at the short, hard end. You cannot change the phrase, so you must exploit the *drawn-out* pronunciation (longer duration = more frames of evidence) and add duration/energy gating.

**3. Architecture: mixednet/MixConv is the right choice; capacity has limits.** microWakeWord uses MixConv mixed depthwise convolutions, adapted from Google's "Streaming Keyword Spotting on Mobile Devices" (Rybakov et al., kws_streaming). The MixedNet defaults are first_conv_filters=32, first_conv_kernel_size=5, stride=3, four MixConv blocks (~64 filters each in the official notebook example), mixconv_kernel_sizes ≈ "[5], [7,11], [9,15], [23]", no residual. BC-ResNet (Kim, Chang, Lee & Sung, "Broadcasted Residual Learning for Efficient Keyword Spotting," Interspeech 2021, DOI 10.21437/Interspeech.2021-383, Qualcomm AI Research) achieves 96.6% on Google Speech Commands v1 with <10k parameters (96.9% on v2; scaled-up BC-ResNet-8 reaches 98.0%/98.7% SOTA) and is the strongest small-footprint alternative, but it is not natively wired into microWakeWord's streaming/quantization pipeline; porting it is high-effort. Your move from v1 (26k) to v3b (32k, 5 blocks) already helped conversational speech (DiPCo 94.6% vs 85%); going much larger fights your tensor_arena and INT8 budget.

**4. Loss functions help class imbalance but are second-order.** Focal loss down-weights easy negatives via (1−p_t)^γ (γ=2 typical) and is documented for KWS imbalance; "Re-Weighted Interval Loss" (Zhang et al., Interspeech 2020) and focal-loss-for-KWS work specifically target false-alarm reduction. AM-softmax / large-margin and metric-learning ("Text Anchor Based Metric Learning," arXiv 2108.05516) improve embedding separation. BUT microWakeWord's existing machinery (per-dataset `penalty_weight`, `positive_class_weight`/`negative_class_weight`, and the two-stage "minimize FAPH then maximize recall" selection) already implements re-weighted training; swapping in focal loss requires patching `train.py` and yields incremental gains versus data work.

**5. Hard-negatives (confusables) are proven to work in *this exact framework*.** A community fork (malonestar/custom-micro-wake-word-model) added TTS-generated "confusable negatives" of phonetically-near phrases as high-penalty hard negatives and documented results: "hey frank" v3 reached 0.414 FA/hr at 97% recall with confusables (sampling 8.0, penalty 5.0) vs heavy false positives without them; v5 reached 0.103 FA/hr best-min at 97.58% recall. Critically, their v4 ablation shows over-weighting (negative_class_weight [60,75], dinner_party sampling 15/penalty 3) made BOTH metrics worse (0.620 FA/hr, 94.5% recall). For «Захар», generate сахар, загар, пожар, комар, Захаров, захват, закат, базар, кошмар, гусар as confusables.

**6. Positives: real recordings beat more TTS speakers.** The malonestar history shows adding 217 real recordings (sampling 8.0, penalty 2.0) to the hey_m5 model materially improved real-world robustness; Home Assistant's Wake Word Collective collected over 5,800 samples covering 30 languages and cut false-rejects from 18% to 5% for "Okay Nabu" (HA blog, 2024-10-24: "our model trained on this data falsely rejects 5% of samples, while previous models rejected 18%"). Your 621 real «Захар» recordings are an asset; more real recordings (especially of the drawn-out form, varied distance/room/speaker) will lift recall on unseen voices more than adding TTS engines. If you do add synthetic variety, Russian options beyond your 4 Piper voices include Silero v5_ru (multiple speakers, CPU-friendly), Vosk-TTS multispeaker Russian (alphacep/vosk-tts-ru-multi), and XTTS-ru.

**7. Data-efficient mining (Amazon approach).** "Towards Data-Efficient Modeling for Wake Word Spotting" (arXiv 2010.06659) starts from 10.18 hours of close-talk "Amazon" wake-word audio (CTM10K, 9,487 utterances) and uses (a) multi-condition training with stratified augmentation and (b) semi-supervised mining of wake-word and *adversarial* examples from an untranscribed corpus, enlarging the set 20–100×. This is directly applicable: run your best current model over a large Russian speech/podcast corpus and harvest its false triggers as hard negatives, and harvest near-positives.

**8. Post-processing and two-stage detection are cheap, high-impact, no retraining of the phrase.** The ESPHome `micro_wake_word` component supports a VAD model ("Enable a Voice Activity Detection model to reduce false accepts from non-speech sounds") that takes the *maximum* probability across the sliding window, plus per-model `probability_cutoff` and `sliding_window_size`. (The analogous openWakeWord VAD uses a Silero VAD gated by a `vad_threshold` argument, default 0.5, which "will only allow a positive prediction… when the VAD model simultaneously has a score above the specified threshold, which can significantly reduce false-positive activations in the presence of non-speech noise.") "To Wake-up or Not to Wake-up: Reducing Keyword False Alarm by Successive Refinement" (Saidutta, Srinivasa et al., Samsung Research America, ICASSP 2023, arXiv 2304.03416) shows a speech?→keyword-like?→which-keyword cascade reduces FA "by up to a factor of 8 on in-domain held-out FA data, and up to a factor of 7 on out-of-domain (OOD) FA data," across models from 13K to 2.41M parameters. openWakeWord's "custom verifier model" (a lightweight logistic-regression second stage) is the same idea.

## Details — Prioritized, Concrete Steps

Each step: **what → expected effect on recall↔music-FAPH → cost/risk → source/config**. Ordered by ROI given your constraints (INT8 ~30k params, CPU-only training, «Захар» fixed).

### Tier 1 — Do these first (highest ROI, fits all constraints)

**Step 1. Add a VAD pre-gate in ESPHome (zero training cost).**
- *Do:* Add the official `vad` model to your `micro_wake_word` YAML. It computes the max probability over the sliding window and blocks wake-word acceptance when speech is absent — exactly the case for instrumental/continuous music. (openWakeWord's equivalent is a Silero VAD gated at `vad_threshold` 0.5.)
- *Effect:* Large music-FAPH reduction with ~0 recall cost (music without speech-like structure is gated out before the KWS decision). Recall on real voice is unaffected because real «Захар» is speech.
- *Cost/risk:* Tiny extra tensor_arena for the VAD model; the ESP32-S3 already runs 4 models concurrently. Risk: music *with* vocals can still pass the VAD.
- *Source:* ESPHome micro_wake_word docs (`vad` option; "reduce false accepts from non-speech sounds"); openWakeWord README (vad_threshold).

**Step 2. Massively expand and re-balance the *continuous-music* negative set, sampled as ambient/split.**
- *Do:* Add MUSAN music (660 music files of different genres within its ~109 h corpus, 16 kHz WAV, CC/US-Public-Domain; arXiv 1510.08484), MTG-Jamendo, and FMA-large to your negatives, generated as long `_ambient` clips with `truncation_strategy: split` (overlapping 100 ms-stride windows — the framework's streaming-FAPH simulation). Target tens of hours of *music specifically*, since music is your failure mode. Keep dinner_party for conversational FA.
- *Effect:* Shifts the whole frontier outward (the reference models' sub-0.1 FAPH comes from tens of thousands of hours of negatives including ~1,000+ h of dedicated music). Diminishing returns set in once music genre/instrument coverage saturates; prioritize *diversity* (genres, instruments, vocals) over raw repetition.
- *Cost/risk:* Disk + CPU feature-generation time (you have plenty). Risk: if you raise music `sampling_weight`/`penalty_weight` too high you suppress recall — keep penalty moderate (see Step 4).
- *Source:* MUSAN (arXiv 1510.08484, OpenSLR); MTG-Jamendo; FMA. HF `kahrendt/microwakeword` for the `split` strategy on `_ambient`.

**Step 3. Generate phonetic confusable hard-negatives for «Захар» (proven in this framework).**
- *Do:* TTS-generate сахар, загар, пожар, комар, Захаров, захват, закат, базар, кошмар, гусар (drawn-out and normal) via your Piper + Silero/Vosk voices. Add as a negative feature set with **sampling_weight ≈ 8.0, penalty_weight ≈ 5.0** (the malonestar values that gave 0.10–0.41 FA/hr at ~97% recall). Use IPA/phoneme input so the final consonants are rendered consistently.
- *Effect:* Sharpens the decision boundary against near-words with minimal recall cost *if* the confusables are genuinely distinct from «Захар». You already see near-zero FA on «Сахар» — extend that to the broader rhyme family.
- *Cost/risk:* If a confusable is *too* close (the malonestar "i'm tired/fired" case cost ~5% recall), it costs recall. Spot-check that synthesized confusables don't sound like «Захар».
- *Source:* malonestar/custom-micro-wake-word-model training history (v3/v5 results; v4 over-correction warning).

**Step 4. Re-tune class/penalty weights using the two-stage selector — but avoid over-suppression.**
- *Do:* Keep microWakeWord's "minimize ambient_false_positives_per_hour to target, then maximize recall" selection. Set `negative_class_weight` in the [40,50]–[50,60] range, `positive_class_weight` [2,2], and set `target_minimization` to your real music-FAPH goal (e.g. 0.5–1.0 FA/hr) rather than 0. Do **not** push negative_class_weight to [60,75].
- *Effect:* Moves you along the frontier in a controlled way. The documented failure mode (v4: [60,75], dinner_party 15/3) worsened both recall (94.5%) and FAPH (0.62) vs the gentler v3/v5.
- *Cost/risk:* Pure hyperparameter search; CPU-cheap to sweep.
- *Source:* malonestar config table; microWakeWord README (penalty_weight/class weights; "increase the negative_class_weight to reduce false acceptances").

**Step 5. Tune the streaming post-processor against a continuous-music test set.**
- *Do:* Build a held-out *continuous music* eval (hours of varied music) and sweep `probability_cutoff`, `sliding_window_size`, and cooldown specifically on it, alongside your 93 honest held-out positives. The official okay_nabu v2 manifest uses a high cutoff (probability_cutoff 0.97, sliding_window_size 5, feature_step_size 10). Larger sliding window + higher cutoff trade latency/recall for fewer FAs; because «Захар» is drawn-out you can afford a slightly larger window (more frames of consistent evidence) which disproportionately hurts short spurious music triggers.
- *Effect:* Directly reshapes the operating point without retraining. Exploits the drawn-out form as a duration prior.
- *Cost/risk:* None to training. Too-high cutoff/window raises false-rejects and latency.
- *Source:* ESPHome docs (cutoff/sliding_window semantics; "A small value lowers latency but may increase the number of false accepts"); official okay_nabu v2 manifest values.

### Tier 2 — Strong, moderate effort

**Step 6. Adversarial false-trigger mining from a large Russian corpus (Amazon data-efficient method).**
- *Do:* Run your current best model over many hours of untranscribed Russian audio (podcasts, radio, Common Voice ru, audiobooks, *and* music streams). Collect every high-probability window that is NOT «Захар» as a hard negative; retrain with these at high penalty. Iterate.
- *Effect:* Targets exactly the inputs the model currently confuses with «Захар» (including specific musical phrases), shifting the frontier where it matters. Amazon enlarged training data 20–100× from 10 h this way.
- *Cost/risk:* Pipeline work; CPU inference over a big corpus is slow but feasible. Risk of mining true near-positives as negatives — filter by a margin.
- *Source:* "Towards Data-Efficient Modeling for Wake Word Spotting," arXiv 2010.06659.

**Step 7. Add a lightweight second-stage verifier (successive refinement).**
- *Do:* After the tiny KWS fires, run a small second classifier (speech-vs-music, or a «Захар»-verifier) on the buffered window before accepting. Can be the VAD (Step 1) or a slightly larger gated model that only runs on candidate triggers (so average compute stays low).
- *Effect:* Up to 7–8× FA reduction reported across 13K–2.4M-param models with no change to the wake phrase. Because it runs only on triggers, recall is preserved if the second-stage threshold is set leniently.
- *Cost/risk:* More flash/RAM and engineering; ESPHome supports multiple concurrent models on the S3.
- *Source:* arXiv 2304.03416 (Successive Refinement, ICASSP 2023); openWakeWord custom verifier model.

**Step 8. Add more *real* positive recordings of the drawn-out «Захар».**
- *Do:* Collect more real recordings (varied speakers, distance, rooms, background) of the drawn-out form; weight them sampling ~8.0 / penalty ~2.0. Prioritize real over additional TTS engines for recall on unseen voices.
- *Effect:* Lifts recall on real voices, partially offsetting any recall cost from Steps 2–4 — i.e., pushes the frontier up on the recall axis.
- *Cost/risk:* Data-collection effort. Diminishing returns vs. negatives once a few hundred varied real clips exist.
- *Source:* malonestar hey_m5_v3 (real recordings improved real-world recall); HA Wake Word Collective (18%→5% false-reject from real samples).

### Tier 3 — Optional / higher-effort, lower marginal ROI

**Step 9. Multi-condition / stratified augmentation tuning.** Increase RIR (MIT) and AddBackgroundNoise probabilities and widen SNR range (community config: AddBackgroundNoise p=0.85, RIR p=0.60, SNR ceiling ~20 dB, GainTransition p=0.25). Effect: robustness to real acoustics; modest. Source: Amazon stratified augmentation; malonestar Cell 7.

**Step 10. Swap cross-entropy for focal loss (γ≈2) or interval/AM-softmax.** Effect: incremental FA reduction under imbalance; requires patching microWakeWord `train.py`. Source: focal-loss-for-KWS; Re-Weighted Interval Loss (Interspeech 2020); arXiv 2108.05516. Lower priority because penalty/class weighting already addresses imbalance.

**Step 11. Knowledge distillation from a larger teacher.** Train a large non-streaming «Захар» teacher (you have CPU+RAM), distill soft labels into the 30k INT8 student. Effect: literature reports students reaching ~95% of teacher accuracy at ~10× fewer params; "Low-resource Low-footprint Wake-word Detection using Knowledge Distillation" (arXiv 2207.03331) shows gains in the low-data regime. Cost: significant pipeline work; not natively in microWakeWord. Keep QAT (microWakeWord already quantizes to INT8 with "typically no reduction in accuracy").

**Step 12. Architecture experiments (BC-ResNet / TC-ResNet).** Only if Tiers 1–2 plateau. BC-ResNet-1 gives 96.6% GSC at <10k params; TC-ResNet is efficient streaming. Both require porting into the microWakeWord streaming+quant pipeline — high effort, uncertain payoff vs. data work.

## Recommendations (staged)

1. **This week (no retraining):** Add the VAD gate (Step 1) and re-tune `probability_cutoff`/`sliding_window_size`/cooldown on a new continuous-music eval set (Step 5). Benchmark: if music-FAPH drops below ~2–4/hr at ≥0.80 recall, proceed; if recall craters, back off the cutoff.
2. **Next:** Expand music negatives (MUSAN/MTG-Jamendo/FMA-large as split-ambient) and add «Захар» confusables at sampling 8 / penalty 5; re-run the two-stage selector with negative_class_weight [50,60], target_minimization ≈ 0.5–1.0 (Steps 2–4). Benchmark: aim for ≤1 music-FAPH at ≥0.85 recall on the 93 honest held-out. **Threshold to change course:** if recall on real held-out drops below ~0.80, lower penalty/negative_class_weight rather than adding more music.
3. **Then:** Adversarial-mine false triggers from a large Russian + music corpus (Step 6) and add more real drawn-out positives (Step 8). Benchmark: each mining round should cut music-FAPH without lowering held-out recall; stop when a round yields <10% relative FAPH improvement (point of diminishing returns).
4. **If still short:** Add the second-stage verifier (Step 7) — this is the most reliable way to get a large additional FA cut for a short fixed phrase. Only after this, consider focal loss / distillation / architecture swaps (Steps 10–12).

## Caveats
- **Hour counts for microWakeWord are not officially published.** The ~31,000-hour figure (incl. ~1,000 h FMA music) is openWakeWord's documented negative-data scale; microWakeWord's HF dataset card lists *composition* (FMA-medium, FSD50K, WHAM, CHiME6/DiPCo, LibriSpeech, VOiCES) but **no per-source hour totals**. Treat "tens of hours of music" as a directional target, not a proven threshold.
- **The malonestar numbers are community results for English "hey frank"/"hey m5," not «Захар».** They are the best available in-framework evidence for confusable-negative weighting, but a 2-syllable Russian word may need different weights — sweep them.
- **Industry syllable/FAPH claims are largely vendor statements** (SoundHound, Picovoice, FutureBee); a FutureBee claim that two-syllable wake words achieve <0.01% false-accepts is marketing, not a peer-reviewed benchmark. The robust, citable result on phrase length is the Google "Wake word evaluation" patent's phonetic-distance framework plus the general consensus that more syllables/phonemes lower FA.
- **tensor_arena discrepancy:** the okay_nabu v2 manifest value appears as 22860 bytes in ESPHome docs but 26080 in an ESPHome issue; confirm against the live JSON before sizing your own arena.
- **Successive-refinement 7–8× figures** (arXiv 2304.03416) are on the authors' FA datasets; your music-specific gain may differ, but the direction (large FA cut, preserved recall) is well-supported.
- **Licensing:** FMA/AudioSet/MIT-RIR and MUSAN have mixed/CC licenses; models trained on them should be treated as non-commercial/personal per the microWakeWord and malonestar notices.
